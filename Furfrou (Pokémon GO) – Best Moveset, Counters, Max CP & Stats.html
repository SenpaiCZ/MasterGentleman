    Furfrou's maximum Combat Power stat is 2387 CP, and its best Pokémon GO moves are Sucker Punch and Grass Knot. Furfrou encounters are boosted during Partly Cloudy weather. Shiny Furfrou is available! ✨

        PvE Analysis

        The Fashionista Furfrou has been a staple of Research Breakthroughs in Pokémon GO, much to the dismay of Trainers. It is terrible for PvE, so just collect its various forms for the Dex and let it be. 😂

        ⚔️ Raids

        Normal types aren't suitable as Raid counters due to a lack of super-effective coverage. The thing is, even among Normal types, Furfrou is bad. Really bad. 😛

        Who let the Furfrou into the Gym? Out. OUT NOW!! 😡

        PvP Analysis

        Sand Attack, Take Down, Sucker Punch, Bite, Dark Pulse, Surf, Grass Knot. My goodness, this stylish doggo has a variety of moves. 🤯

        Style… but no substance. Give it a treat and order it to stay away from PvP. 🐩

        🔵 Great League

        It has a Dark-type moveset and, being a Normal-type Pokémon, it heavily resists Ghost-type damage. That's a niche, yep, but it's a terrible PvP Pokémon in all other matchups. 😐

        Mega and Primal Raid Bonus –Mega Pokémon boost the attack power of all other Pokémon when battling in Raid Battles by 10%. Attacks that share their type with the Mega Pokémon's type(s) receive a 30% boost. However, these bonuses do not stack if there are multiple Mega-Evolved Pokémon battling simultaneously; only the higher boost will be applied.

        Mega and Primal Catch Bonus –While a Pokémon is Mega-Evolved, additional XP and Candy is granted when catching a Pokémon that shares a type with it, or for any raid boss caught. These do not stack with each other. Additionally, High and Max Level Mega-Evolved Pokémon grant an increased chance to obtain Candy XL when catching a Pokémon that shares a type with it.

        Note on Primal Kyogre, Primal Groudon, and Mega Rayquaza
        • Primal Kyogre boosts Water-, Electric- and Bug-type attacks and Pokémon.
        • Primal Groudon boosts Fire-, Grass- and Ground-type attacks and Pokémon.
        • Mega Rayquaza boosts Flying-, Dragon- and Psychic-type attacks and Pokémon.

                Catch probability with multiple throws

                In order to help Trainers understand their overall odds when catching Furfrou, we enable the functionality to showing the total chance to catch it depending on the number of available throws. We calculate this chance as if you repeated the same throw N-number of times.

                To activate this functionality, change the number of Number of throws to a value greater than 1. The Number of throws option is available in the configuration options above the chart

                Catch rate formula

                Pokémon GO catch rate formula can be expressed as follows:

                • CATCH_MULTIPLIERS = ball * curve * berry * throwType * (type1Medal + type2Medal / 2)
                • CATCH_RATE = 1 - Math.pow(1 - baseCatchRate / (2 * cpm), CATCH_MULTIPLIERS)

                There are different multipliers that come into play when calculating catch rate.

                Curved throws are more effective than regular throws, stronger types of Poké Ball have a higher catch rate, and using a Razz Berry or a Golden Razz Berry also improves your chances.
